Cost and Fairness Model

GPUs are enforced using GPU-seconds, not fractional GPUs.

Why GPU-seconds

You cannot reliably carve a GPU into fractions for arbitrary workloads. Instead, fairness is enforced over time.

  • Each tenant earns credits at a fixed rate (token bucket).
  • Jobs consume credits when claimed.
  • If a tenant runs out of credits, the job is delayed (backoff) instead of poisoning the execution queue.

ROI & Business Impact

By enforcing strict GPU-seconds quotas rather than allocating full GPUs per tenant:

  1. Utilization: Increases GPU bin-packing density by running smaller jobs in idle gaps, reducing wasted accelerator time.
  2. Predictability: Tenants are capped by spend rate, eliminating surprise cloud bills and making cost envelopes enforceable.
  3. Risk Mitigation: Runaway or recursive agent loops are terminated automatically when the token bucket empties, preventing financial leakage.

What this enforces

  • Predictable cost per tenant
  • Controlled bursts (via burst caps)
  • Prevention of noisy-neighbor monopolies
  • Budget-safe horizontal scaling
This aligns technical execution with financial reality.
