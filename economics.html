Economics & ROI

AI doesn’t get expensive because it scales. It gets expensive because it scales without enforcement.

Why GPU-seconds

GPUs cannot be reliably sliced for arbitrary workloads. Instead of allocating fractions of hardware, this system enforces fairness over time.

  • Each tenant earns credits at a fixed rate (token bucket).
  • Jobs consume credits when they are claimed.
  • If credits are insufficient, jobs are delayed instead of failing or poisoning the queue.

What this enforces

  • Predictable spend per tenant
  • Controlled burst behavior
  • Noisy-neighbor protection
  • Automatic containment of runaway agents

Business impact

  • Higher utilization through better bin-packing and fewer idle gaps
  • Predictable cost curves instead of surprise GPU bills
  • Built-in risk mitigation for recursive or misconfigured agents

How this maps to the system

  • Quota enforcement is done atomically at job claim time.
  • Workers only execute once the control plane grants a leased right-to-run.
  • Delayed execution preserves fairness without dropping work.
